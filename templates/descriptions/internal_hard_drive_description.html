Storage Specifications

Storage devices are usually compared by capacity, interface, physical size, and caching. These factors affect how much data you can store and how quickly the drive can transfer it.

  • Capacity: The total storage space available.
  • Type: The storage technology, such as HDD or SSD.
  • Interface: The connection standard used by the drive, such as SATA or NVMe.
  • Form Factor: The physical size and mounting style of the drive.
  • Cache: Temporary high-speed memory used to improve access performance.
