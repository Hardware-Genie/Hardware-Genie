Video Card Specifications

Graphics cards are typically judged by memory capacity, clock speed, physical size, and cooling design. These specs help compare gaming, rendering, and general graphics performance.

  • Memory: The onboard VRAM capacity available for textures, frame buffers, and GPU workloads.
  • Core Clock: The GPU's base operating speed.
  • Boost Clock: The maximum boosted speed the card can sustain when conditions allow.
  • Chipset: The GPU model or family that determines the card's overall capability.
  • Length: The physical length of the card, important for case compatibility.
  • Color: The exterior finish and style of the cooler and shroud.
