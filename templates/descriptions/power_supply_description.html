Power Supply Specifications

Power supplies should be compared by wattage, efficiency, modularity, and connector support. The right PSU keeps a build stable and leaves room for future upgrades.

  • Wattage: The maximum power output the unit can deliver.
  • Efficiency: How effectively the PSU converts wall power into usable DC power.
  • Modular: Whether the cables are fully modular, semi-modular, or fixed.
  • Form Factor: The PSU size standard, usually ATX for desktop systems.
  • Color: The exterior finish, which can matter for visible case builds.
